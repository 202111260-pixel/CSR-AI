Project Description

This initiative provides solar-powered electricity to 420 rural households in the Bahla governorate that previously lacked reliable access to the national grid. The project covers installation of 1,200 photovoltaic panels, battery storage units, and micro-grid infrastructure. Expected to reduce diesel generator usage by 90% and cut household energy costs by an average of OMR 85 per month. The project also includes capacity-building workshops for local technicians to ensure long-term operational sustainability.

◎
Objectives
  1. 1
    Provide clean electricity to 420 rural households in Bahla district
  2. 2
    Reduce reliance on diesel generators by at least 90%
  3. 3
    Train 30 local technicians in solar panel maintenance
  4. 4
    Lower household energy costs by minimum OMR 85/month
  5. 5
    Achieve zero-carbon energy production for participating villages
✓
Expected Outputs
1,200 photovoltaic panels installed and operational
420 households connected to solar micro-grid
3 battery storage facilities constructed
30 certified solar technicians trained
Community energy management committee established
Annual impact report published for 3 consecutive years
